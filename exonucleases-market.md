<p><p>The exonucleases market can be categorized into two main types based on their energy requirements. ATP-dependent exonucleases utilize adenosine triphosphate (ATP) as an energy source for their nucleolytic activity, playing crucial roles in various cellular processes such as DNA repair and degradation. Conversely, ATP-independent exonucleases function without an external energy supply, relying instead on inherent enzymatic mechanisms to catalyze DNA or RNA degradation. Both types are vital for maintaining nucleic acid integrity and regulating cellular processes.</p></p>
