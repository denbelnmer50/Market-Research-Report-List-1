<p><p>The cholesterol blood test market is categorized into two primary types: fingerstick blood tests and venous blood tests. Fingerstick blood tests involve collecting a small drop of blood from a fingertip, offering convenience and quick results, often used for at-home testing. In contrast, venous blood tests involve drawing blood from a vein, providing a comprehensive lipid profile and greater accuracy, typically conducted in clinical settings. Both methods cater to different user preferences and healthcare settings in monitoring cholesterol levels.</p></p>
