<p><ul><li>Non-Emulsion Type</li><li>Emulsion Type</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The all-vegetable shortening market is categorized into two main types: non-emulsion and emulsion types. Non-emulsion shortening is typically solid, providing a stable texture and is often used in baking for its ability to create flaky layers. Emulsion shortening, on the other hand, combines fats with water to achieve a creamy, spreadable texture, making it suitable for applications like frosting or certain baked goods. Each type serves distinct culinary purposes, influencing product choice in various food production contexts.</p></p>
