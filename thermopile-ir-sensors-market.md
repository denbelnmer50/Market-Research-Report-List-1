<p><p>Thermopile infrared (IR) sensors are categorized into two main types based on their mounting methods: through-hole and surface mount device (SMD). Through-hole thermopile sensors are designed for traditional circuit boards, featuring leads that pass through holes, offering durability and ease of handling in various applications. In contrast, SMD thermopiles are compact and designed for direct mounting on the surface of printed circuit boards, allowing for a more efficient use of space and facilitating automated assembly processes. Both types serve diverse applications in temperature measurement and environmental monitoring.</p></p>
