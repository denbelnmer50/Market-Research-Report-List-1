<p><p>The friction welding market comprises three main types: linear friction welding, rotary friction welding, and stir friction welding. Linear friction welding involves oscillating two components along a linear path under pressure, creating heat through friction to forge a bond. Rotary friction welding, on the other hand, rotates one component against another, generating heat at the contact surface. Stir friction welding mixes materials together by rotating a tool to generate heat, allowing for strong joints in various applications. Each method offers unique advantages for different materials and industries.</p></p>
