<p><p>Food encapsulation involves encasing active ingredients to enhance their stability, bioavailability, and controlled release. Microencapsulation uses tiny capsules to protect and release these ingredients gradually, making it ideal for flavors and nutrients. Nanoencapsulation utilizes nanoscale materials for improved delivery and absorption, often targeting specific sites in the body. Hybrid encapsulation combines both micro and nano technologies, optimizing the advantages of each method for enhanced functionality. These technologies cater to the growing demand for health-oriented and functional food products.</p></p>
