<p><p>The veterinary disinfectant market comprises various types, including iodine and iodophors, which are effective against a broad range of pathogens. Chlorhexidine is favored for its residual activity and skin compatibility. Alcohol, often used for surface disinfection, acts quickly but may evaporate rapidly. Hydrogen peroxide offers strong oxidative properties, making it versatile for both surfaces and equipment. Other disinfectants may include quaternary ammonium compounds or peracetic acid, catering to specific veterinary needs and ensuring enhanced hygiene in animal care settings.</p></p>
